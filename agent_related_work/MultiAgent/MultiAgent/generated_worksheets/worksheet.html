Answer Key 🔑

🌳 Question 1 Answer:

Answer: Sunlight and Water.

Solution:

Step 1: The scenario mentions the sapling gets 'bright morning sun' and Rahim 'waters it carefully'.

Step 2: These two elements, sunlight and water, are essential for plants to make their own food through photosynthesis.

🍆 Question 2 Answer:

Answer: The process is Photosynthesis (সালোকসংশ্লেষণ), and the green substance is Chlorophyll (ক্লোরোফিল).

Solution:

Step 1: The process by which plants make their own food is called Photosynthesis.

Step 2: The green color in plant leaves is due to a substance called Chlorophyll, which is crucial for capturing sunlight during photosynthesis.

🌳 Question 3 Answer:

Answer: Oxygen (অক্সিজেন).

Solution:

Step 1: Plants perform photosynthesis, taking in carbon dioxide and releasing another gas.

Step 2: The gas released that is vital for living things to breathe and makes the air feel fresher is Oxygen.

🍚 Question 4 Answer:

Answer: The invisible thing from the air is Carbon dioxide (কার্বন ডাই অক্সাইড). The thing from the ground is Water. The green substance in their leaves is Chlorophyll (ক্লোরোফিল).

Solution:

Step 1: Plants absorb water from the ground (soil) through their roots.

Step 2: Plants take an invisible gas called carbon dioxide from the air.

Step 3: The green pigment in their leaves, called chlorophyll, is responsible for capturing sunlight, which is also needed for the process.

💧☀️ Question 5 Answer:

Answer: Water and sunlight are two essential components for photosynthesis. Plants absorb water from the soil and use the energy from sunlight, along with carbon dioxide, to produce their own food (glucose) and grow.

Solution:

Step 1: Plants need raw materials and energy to make food.

Step 2: Water is absorbed by the roots from the soil and is a necessary raw material for photosynthesis.

Step 3: Sunlight provides the energy required for the chemical reaction of photosynthesis, converting carbon dioxide and water into food.

🌬️🐄 Question 6 Answer:

Answer: Plants like the banyan tree perform photosynthesis, a process where they take in carbon dioxide and release oxygen into the air. This oxygen is vital for humans and animals (like the cow) to breathe and survive, thus making the air feel 'fresher' and supporting all life.

Solution:

Step 1: Plants conduct photosynthesis, absorbing carbon dioxide from the air.

Step 2: During this process, they release oxygen as a byproduct.

Step 3: Oxygen is the gas that humans and animals need to breathe for their survival, which contributes to fresher air.

🍌🥭 Question 7 Answer:

Answer: The four essential components Nabil's plants need are: Carbon dioxide, Water, Sunlight, and Chlorophyll. The two important products they create are: Glucose (food) and Oxygen.

Solution:

Step 1: Identify the inputs needed for photosynthesis: 'a gas from the air' is carbon dioxide, 'water', 'sunlight', and 'green leaves' imply chlorophyll.

Step 2: Identify the outputs of photosynthesis: 'make their own food' is glucose, and 'release a gas that helps humans and animals survive' is oxygen.

🌳🦌 Question 8 Answer:

Answer: Mangrove trees grow tall and strong by performing photosynthesis. In this process, they use sunlight, water, carbon dioxide (from the air), and chlorophyll (in their green leaves) to produce their own food (glucose). This food provides the energy and building blocks for the trees to grow. This process is crucial not only for the trees because the food they make sustains their life and growth, but also for animals like the deer. The deer eat the leaves and twigs of these mangrove trees, obtaining the energy and nutrients from the food the trees produced. Without photosynthesis by the mangrove trees, the deer would not have a food source and could not survive, highlighting its importance for the entire Sundarbans ecosystem.

Solution:

Step 1: Explain how plants grow tall and strong: by making their own food through photosynthesis using sunlight, water, carbon dioxide, and chlorophyll.

Step 2: Explain the importance for the plants themselves: the food provides energy for their growth and survival.

Step 3: Explain the importance for other living organisms (like deer): animals eat plants to get the energy and nutrients from the food produced by photosynthesis, thus forming the base of the food chain.
